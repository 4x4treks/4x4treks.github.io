18 Day
Gulf Savannah Explorer





Experience the mystery and remoteness of the Gulf Country on this amazing Gulf Savannah Trek. Exclusive access to remote cattle stations, amazing camping locations, secluded swimming holes, iconic locations, barra fishing gear and tuition and amazing scenery. Be secure in the knowledge that everything is arranged for you and you're travelling with an experienced and well prepared guide and a group of like-minded people.

Starting in Townsville and ending in Mataranka NT, we'll take you to the best locations, let you know when hazards are coming up and help you to safely tackle the extensive Outback road driving.

Included - 4 evening pub meals, all camping fees and Parks Permits (National Parks, farmstays, caravan parks incl. final night at Mataranka Bitter Springs), guided tour at the Undara Lava Tubes and Cobbold Gorge, Karumba, Lawn Hill NP (and some other locations that will remain secret), 4WD instruction, tyre changing and mechanical assistance, hot shower, qualified and knowledgeable guide, reliable 200 Series Landcruiser tour guide vehicle, all safety, recovery, communications and emergency equipment (incl. Garmin inReach Tracker/PLB with satellite texting enabled).

Gulf Savannah FAQs

 Experienced & qualified tour guide 

Your guide Tony is not only an experienced remote area tour guide, he's also a Cert IV qualified trainer and assessor in four wheel driving. And he's a friendly bloke too!

Vehicle and Trailer Requirements

You will need a high clearance 4WD in good mechanical order with low range and light truck (LT) tyres with at least 50% tread remaining. Heavy duty shocks and springs are required for this adventure. We will experience rough, corrugated roads with bulldust holes and standard suspension will not last.

An off-road trailer or caravan needs to have independent suspension, good ground clearance and be sturdy enough to survive many days on corrugated roads. Discuss your trailer with us before booking to ensure its suitability. Full details of specs, setup and spares is given on booking.


 Mechanical assistance

Get the practical mechanical help you need if things don't go to plan. From tyre changes to changing shocks (and a lot more). 

 Supplies and fuel

While this tour takes you through some of the most isolated areas in Australia, we have regular stops for fuel and food. 600km fuel range is plenty. 2 spare tyres on rims are recommended just in case. It's also a good idea to have a mechanic go over your vehicle and trailer before you head off. You will need a fridge and supplies to last 5 days, as well as camping equipment that is effieicnt to put up and take down.
