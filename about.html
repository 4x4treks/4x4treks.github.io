Meet Tony Davys,
Aus_4X4_Treks

Tony is an accredited Savannah Guides tour guide, EcoGuides Australia qualified, Remote Area First Aid accredited [HLTAID013 Provide First Aid in remote and isolated sites] and 4WD Instructor (Cert IV Trainer & Assessor, and owner of Rockhampton 4WD Training). He has been involved in the 4WD industry for the last 18 years, guiding in Australia for 8 years since moving from New Zealand. With the qualifications of a Degree in Tourism and a Masters Degree in Science specialising in Wildlife Management, he is also a self-confessed "wildlife fanatic".

Tony is an avid outdoorsman, keen photographer and fisherman and very experienced in the backcountry. His love of the outdoors came from growing up on a dairy farm and later, four wheel driving on numerous trips into the mountains of New Zealand’s South Island.

He has had a varied career which has added to his diverse knowledge base. Working for the NZ Department of Conservation deep in the bush on the West Coast of the South Island straight out of Uni was both rewarding and physically challenging. Catching and radio-collaring stoats (like a weasel) and helping to protect vulnerable Kiwi populations was a real highlight of his time on "The Coast".

He then went on to work for (what is now) the Ministry for Primary Industries as a Quarantine Officer (Biosecurity) at Auckland International Airport. The 8 years he spent on the front line dealing with passengers and biosecurity threats taught him a lot about human nature and how to deal with all kinds of people from all over the World. He was also regularly featured on the NZ "Border Patrol" TV series and has some great stories to tell. You can see Tony in his (much) younger days here: [YouTube Link to Border Patrol  45 seconds in].

After living in Auckland for 8 years (too long in the city for a country boy!) he moved to the South Island with Helen, his wife, and took over an existing 4WD Hire business "Overland 4WD Rentals Ltd". Soon after, the twins came along, and 4 years after buying the business in 2010 the Christchurch earthquakes hit and virtually destroyed the business. It wasn't all doom a gloom though. Tony got to meet the most amazing people from all over the World, some becoming life-long friends.  

Due to his business contacts and experience, Tony was offered a job in Australia as a tour guide and 4WD trainer with Great Divide Tours near Canberra. The family jumped at the chance (and jumped the ditch) in January 2015 as Christchurch and the tourism industry had never recovered from the earthquakes. Tony quickly fell in love with the wide open spaces and vast landscapes of the Australian Outback.

Unable to control the urge to run his own business again, Tony moved the family to Rockhampton, QLD in January 2019 and started Aus 4X4 Treks and Rockhampton 4WD Training. 


Meet Michael Alexander,
See Around Corners
[trusted affiliate business]

With over 25 years exploring outback Australia, avoiding bitumen at every turn and putting his Toyota Troopcarrier 4WD to good use, Michael has developed a comprehensive understanding of what is on offer to travellers in Australia.

This passion for touring has evolved into a passion for tour guiding and the creation of See Around Corners, allowing Michael to share his knowledge of hand picked destinations and indigenous culture across the Savannah Way in northern Australia.

Michael is an accredited Savannah Guide, EcoGuide Australia, Kakadu, Uluru - Kata Tjuta and Watarrka Guide, and also has a Cert IV in Training and Assessment (4WD) and a Cert III in Tourism (Guiding).

He is a board member of Savanah Guides and a member of Tour Guides Australia and WAITOC which helps him maintain the highest professional guiding standards.

What is a tag-along tour & why travel with our tag-along group?

SAFE AND HASSLE FREE - LIKE MINDED COMPANIONS
We provide a safe and hassle-free way for people to travel to remote locations. Our carefully planned tours are designed to let you safely experience incredible 4X4 locations with like-minded and enthusiastic companions in places most people would never go by themselves. If things do go astray, we are Remote Area First Aid trained, and carry a defibrillator (AED) in the lead vehicle. We have extensive safety procedures, and can connect to outside help in a matter of seconds, with 3 types of satellite communications.

FOLLOW THE LEADER - NO EXPERIENCE NECESSARY
Very simply put, you follow the tour leader in your own or a hired 4X4 vehicle. And you don't need to have any off-road driving experience because our professional tour guides provide instruction and support throughout each tour to help clients successfully and safely tackle challenging terrain in isolated situations.

LIFE-LONG SKILLS
You will come away from a tour with us with a real sense of achievement and 4X4 skills to last you a lifetime.

LIMITED NUMBERS - VARIED ITINERARIES
We use a network of travel experts to provide a seamless experience for our clients. Up to 8 clients' vehicles come on our tag-along tours which are a mix of self, part and fully catered and accommodated or camping, depending on the location.

EXPERIENCED AND QUALIFIED GUIDES
Our guides are qualified in both tour guiding and 4WD instruction, and have extensive experience in remote area travel.
