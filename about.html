Meet Tony Davys,
Aus_4X4_Treks

Tony is an accredited Savannah Guides tour guide, EcoGuides Australia, and Tour Guides Australia certified guide, Remote Area First Aid accredited [HLTAID013 Provide First Aid in remote and isolated sites] and Cert IV Trainer & Assessor 4WD Instructor. He has been involved in the 4WD industry for the last 21 years, guiding in Australia for 10 years, even before moving from New Zealand. With the qualifications of a Degree in Tourism and a Masters Degree in Science specialising in Wildlife Management, he is also a self-confessed "wildlife fanatic".

Tony is an avid outdoorsman, keen photographer and fisherman and very experienced in the backcountry. His appreciation for the outdoors came about through his farming upbringing and further developed through extensive four wheel driving adventures in the mountains of New Zealand's South Island.

He has had a diverse career that has contributed to his extensive knowledge. Fresh out of university, Tony embarked on a rewarding yet physically challenging role deep in the bush on the West Coast of the South Island, working for the New Zealand Department of Conservation. One of the highlights during this period was capturing and radio-collaring stoats (similar to weasels), as well as aiding in the protection of vulnerable Kiwi populations.

Following that, he transitioned to a role at Auckland International Airport as a Quarantine Officer (Biosecurity) for what is now the Ministry for Primary Industries. During his eight years on the front line, managing passengers and addressing biosecurity threats, he gained valuable insights into human nature and developed the skills to interact with people from diverse backgrounds. He was also regularly featured on the NZ "Border Patrol" TV series and has some great stories to tell. 

Having spent eight years in Auckland (which felt too long for a country boy!), he and his wife Helen decided to make a move to the South Island. There, they took over an established 4WD Hire business called "Overland 4WD Rentals Ltd." Shortly after, their twins arrived. Unfortunately, four years after acquiring the business in 2010, the devastating Christchurch earthquakes struck, nearly destroying the enterprise. Despite the challenges, there were silver linings. Tony had the opportunity to connect with remarkable individuals from around the world, forming lifelong friendships.

Thanks to his business contacts and 4WD expertise, Tony received an enticing job offer in Australia as a tour guide and 4WD trainer with Great Divide Tours near Canberra. In January 2015, the family seized the opportunity, making the move across the ditch, especially since Christchurch and the local tourism industry continued to struggle after the earthquakes. Tony quickly fell in love with the wide open spaces and vast landscapes of the Australian Outback.

Driven by the desire to run his own business once more, Tony relocated the family to Rockhampton, QLD, in January 2019 and launched Australian 4X4 Treks.

Meet Michael Alexander,
See Around Corners
[trusted affiliate business]

With over 25 years exploring outback Australia, avoiding bitumen at every turn and putting his Toyota Troopcarrier 4WD to good use, Michael has developed a comprehensive understanding of what is on offer to travellers in Australia.

This passion for touring has evolved into a passion for tour guiding and the creation of See Around Corners, allowing Michael to share his knowledge of hand picked destinations and indigenous culture in Arnhem Land and the Kimberley in Northern Australia.

Michael is an accredited Savannah Guide, EcoGuide Australia, Kakadu, Uluru - Kata Tjuta and Watarrka Guide, and also has a Cert IV in Training and Assessment (4WD) and a Cert III in Tourism (Guiding).

He is a board member of Savanah Guides and a member of Tour Guides Australia and WAITOC which helps him maintain the highest professional guiding standards.

Section 5: Booking and Cancellation Policy

DEPOSITS
• To make a booking, you must pay a non-refundable deposit of 25% of the total tour price. Payment may be made by credit card through our online Xero invoicing system or by direct bank deposit. Credit card payments will incur a handling fee, which will be disclosed at the time of booking.

• If you cannot make the tour for medical reasons, we will provide you with a credit of the deposit amount to be used on another of our tours departing within 16 months of your original departure date. Note that the tour price may be different at the time of re-booking and an additional fee may apply, and we will require a medical certificate from a doctor.

• A booking is accepted when we issue written confirmation to you, generally by email.

BALANCE PAYMENTS
• The balance payment must be received by us in full at least 40 days before the start date of the tour.

• If a tour is cancelled due to unforeseen and uncontrollable circumstances, where we are not at fault, such as a bushfire or flooding, a force majeure clause is enacted. This means that any money paid towards your booking is credited to be used on another of our tours departing within 16 months of your original departure date, less any non-refundable deposits we have paid on your behalf  (to a third party supplier e.g. accommodation, scenic flight operator).

• If a tour is cancelled by us, for example, due to insufficient bookings or illness, we will refund all amounts paid by you, minus our non-refundable deposit, and any non-refundable deposits we have paid on your behalf, and your deposit will be credited to be used on another of our tours departing within 16 months of the original tour's departure date.

• If you cancel your tour, you must notify us in writing immediately. If your notification is received 41 days or more before the tour is scheduled to begin, we will refund any amounts paid by you, minus the non-refundable 25% deposit, and any non-refundable deposits we have paid on your behalf, and any reasonable administration expenses. If your notification is received 40 days or less before departure, no refund will be given.

• Notwithstanding the clause above, we will provide you with a credit to be used on another of our tours departing within 16 months of your original departure date if you cannot attend the tour for medical reasons, and a medical certificate is supplied to us. Note that the tour price may be different at the time of re-booking, meaning an additional payment may be required.

• If you choose to withdraw from the tour before its completion, regardless of the reason, no refund will be issued.

What is Personal Information and why do we collect it?
Personal Information is information or an opinion that identifies an individual. We don’t guarantee website links or policy of authorised third parties.

We collect your Personal Information for the primary purpose of providing our services to you, providing information to our suppliers and marketing. We may also use your Personal Information for secondary purposes closely related to the primary purpose, in circumstances where you would reasonably expect such use or disclosure. You may unsubscribe from our mailing/marketing lists at any time by contacting us in writing. When we collect Personal Information we will, where appropriate and where possible, explain to you why we are collecting the information and how we plan to use it.

Sensitive Information
Sensitive information is defined in the Privacy Act to include information or opinion about such things as an individual's racial or ethnic origin, political opinions, membership of a political association, religious or philosophical beliefs, membership of a trade union or other professional body, criminal record or health information.

Sensitive information will be used by us only:
• For the primary purpose for which it was obtained
• For a secondary purpose that is directly related to the primary purpose

Third Parties
